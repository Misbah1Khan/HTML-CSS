MUTTON NIHARI

Mutton Nihari is a rich and indulgent dish that is steeped in the culinary heritage of Pakistan and India. This slow-cooked delicacy features tender mutton that has been simmered for hours in a thick and flavorful gravy. The mutton is cooked until it becomes incredibly soft and succulent, allowing the meat to absorb the spices and develop a deep, complex flavor. The hallmark of Mutton Nihari lies in its robust and aromatic gravy, which is infused with a blend of spices like ginger, garlic, cinnamon, cloves, and cardamom. These spices impart a tantalizing aroma and a rich, savory taste to the dish. Mutton Nihari is traditionally enjoyed as a special breakfast dish or served as a centerpiece during festive occasions and gatherings. It is often garnished with fried onions, fresh ginger, chopped cilantro, and a squeeze of lemon, adding a burst of freshness to the flavorful gravy. Whether savored with naan bread or paired with fragrant rice, Mutton Nihari is a true culinary delight that showcases the art of slow-cooking and the depth of flavors in Pakistani and Indian cuisine.
